Occupational safety at the enterprise?

The objectives of occupational safety and health are to minimize the likelihood of injury or illness to the employee while ensuring comfort at maximum productivity.

Occupational safety plays an important role in a person's working life. Proper organization of labor significantly increases its productivity and dramatically reduces the possibility of occupational injuries and injuries. This, in turn, has a direct positive impact on the economic side of work: there is a decrease in the cost of sick leave and treatment for employees, the number and amount of compensation for work in harmful conditions decreases, etc.

According to statistical calculations, the cost of necessary measures and means for occupational safety and health is ten times less than the cost of accidents related to their timely failure.

Industrial safety at hazardous production facilities

Industrial safety is the state of protection of the vital interests of the individual and society from accidents at hazardous production facilities and the consequences of these accidents[1].

Industrial safety is not an integral part of occupational safety and health. We can say that these are intersecting sets. The main purpose of industrial safety is to prevent and/or minimize the consequences of accidents at hazardous production facilities.

Accident - destruction of structures and (or) technical devices used at a hazardous production facility, uncontrolled explosion and (or) release of hazardous substances. The main purpose of occupational safety and health is to preserve the life and health of employees. That is, accidents that do not harm the life and health of employees are quite possible, and, conversely, harm to the life and health of employees can be caused without accidents.

Fire safety

Fire safety is the state of protection of an individual, property, society and the state from fires. Ensuring fire safety is one of the most important functions of the state.

Stages of indoor fire

  • For the first 10-20 minutes, the fire spreads linearly along the combustible material. At this time, the room is filled with smoke; it is impossible to see the flame at this time. The air temperature rises in the room to 250-300 degrees. This is the ignition temperature of the main combustible materials.
  • After 20 minutes, the volumetric spread of the fire begins.
  • After another 10 minutes, the glazing collapses. The influx of fresh air is increasing, and the development of the fire is rapidly progressing. The temperature reaches 900 degrees.
  • The burnout phase. The maximum fire rate is within 10 minutes.
  • After the main substances burn out, a fire stabilization phase occurs (from 20 minutes to 5 hours). If the fire cannot spread to other rooms, the fire goes outside.
  • At this time, the burnt-out structures collapse.
